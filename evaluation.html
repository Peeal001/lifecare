Heuristic Evaluation

Improving our design through usability testing

We conducted a heuristic evaluation of our high-fidelity prototype with the help of our peer team "flouri$h". They reviewed our interface using Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics and identified several usability issues. We categorized these issues by heuristic and severity and made targeted design improvements based on their feedback.

Heuristic Issue Severity Fix Implemented
H5: Error Prevention Assuming that users must fill out the same information for every appointment. Could be improved with a profile system that auto-fills forms. 2 Not addressed (not prioritized)
H8: Aesthetic Design Initial designs lacked color and visual appeal in early prototypes. 1 Enhanced hi-fi with color, icons, and improved layout
H10: Help & Documentation System could benefit from additional guidance for target users. 2 Added video tutorial from searching clinics to making appointments

User Testing

In addition to the heuristic evaluation, we conducted user testing with five participants who matched our target demographic. Key findings included:

  • Users appreciated the simplified language and intuitive navigation
  • Some confusion around what documents might be needed for appointments
  • Strong positive reaction to privacy protections
  • Request for additional language options beyond those initially provided

These insights led to further refinements of our prototype, particularly around transparency about documentation requirements and expanding language options.
